# Christopher Alan Homes: Built for Florida, Not Generic

Privately owned, Southwest Florida's largest private builder (ranked #57 nationally), 2024 Small Business of the Year. In Palm Bay, they're building scatter lots with four floor plans (fifth multi-gen design arriving end of 2026). That's the corporate summary. Here's what actually matters.

## The Star: The 3-Door Slider

Three panes of glass as big as two standard sliding doors at most other builders. They accordion from either side and expand to the full width of the opening. When they're open, you've got an unobstructed view of your backyard or pool — light floods the main living area, and the space feels bigger than it actually is. It's the detail that proves they get the climate. For someone who wants to live inside *and* see their outdoor space, it's the feature.

## Lot Strategy — Convenience Over Instagram

They position lots on main roads and accessible corridors, not buried in the back of neighborhoods. Road noise is real. But here's the thing: impact glass handles it. You're trading cul-de-sac aesthetics for actual commute logic. That's a Florida trade-off worth making if you value your time.

## The Impact Window Trade

Single-pane impact windows (like Lennar). Not as energy efficient as dual-pane, but affordable and critically — you can actually see the storm outside instead of boarding up and hiding in a darkened bunker. That's a Florida-first choice, not a cost-cutting shortcut.

## Design Philosophy: Your Blank Canvas

Four floor plans, limited builder options. That's the point. You get a solid foundation — tile in wet areas and living spaces, carpet in bedrooms — and a blank canvas to make it your own. Want to upgrade the kitchen, swap finishes, add custom touches? You're not locked into the builder's ecosystem. Select from the full range of aftermarket upgrades and install them over time instead of paying for them up front. You control the outcome, not a design menu.

## The Bottom Line

Multi-gen design arriving end of 2026. Perfect if you want a well-built Florida home you can actually shape into what you need — positioned where you actually live, not where it photographs best.
