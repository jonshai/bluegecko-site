# Carharp Homes: Attention to Detail That Changes How You Live

When you walk through a Carharp home, something feels different. The spaces feel bigger than their square footage suggests. The finishes feel intentional. The floor plans feel like someone actually thought about how families live.

That's not accident. It's the result of semi-custom building that prioritizes thoughtful design over checkbox amenities.

## The Details That Matter

Carharp builds in the upper $300s, but their homes carry luxury touches you'd expect at significantly higher price points. Steel-frame interior walls provide structural integrity most builders skip. Pantries come with five shelves as standard — not three. Kitchens feature tall cabinetry that actually uses vertical space. Showers have dual shower heads. Tile runs throughout the home, not LVP pretending to be wood.

These aren't add-ons. They're baseline.

## Floor Plans Built for Real Life

The genius is in the layouts. Multi-generational plans have completely separate sections with exterior entries — not just a guest bedroom tacked on. Two-kid bedrooms aren't an afterthought; they're designed with distinct spaces so siblings aren't forced into a single closet. Every floor plan is engineered to make 1,700–2,100 square feet feel spacious in ways you only understand when you walk through.

No wasted space. No oversized master at the expense of everything else. Just homes that work.

## The Customization Window

Carharp typically has 6–10 homes available within the next 90 days. But here's where they separate from production builders: if your timeline extends beyond that, the time to start is *now*. If you negotiate before permits are requested, you can customize beyond their standard plans. Move a wall. Add a door. Lock in the specifics while you still can.

That runway matters. It's the difference between buying what's available and building what is uniquely yours.

## Why It Matters

In Palm Bay and Port St. Lucie's building landscape, Carharp is a boutique builder on the rise — one of the hidden gems you miss if you're only looking at national or regional names. This is a local builder who understands how to build a Florida home that feels at home in Florida. Attention to detail at this price point isn't luxury. It's respect.
